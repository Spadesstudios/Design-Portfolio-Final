Overview

What is Spades?

Spades is an event production group built from the ground up — handling everything from initial concept and venue sourcing to day-of logistics and post-event documentation. The business was founded with the belief that events should be executed with the same precision and intentionality as any world-class product.

As Founder and Creative Director, I was responsible for every layer of the business: establishing the brand, developing operational frameworks, managing client relationships, and directing creative output across all events.

"Great events don't happen by accident — they're engineered, designed, and delivered with intent."

Brand Building

Identity & Visual Language

The Spades brand was built around a core tension: sharp professionalism meeting bold creative energy. The spade suit symbol became the anchor of the visual identity — a mark that communicates precision, edge, and a certain unavoidable confidence.

Brand deliverables I developed included:

  • Full logo system — primary, secondary, and icon marks with usage guidelines
  • Brand color palette, typography hierarchy, and tone-of-voice documentation
  • Marketing collateral including flyers, social templates, and stage graphics
  • Promotional photography and event photography direction
  • Client-facing pitch decks and proposal templates

Operations

Production & Project Management

Running events requires tight project management — coordinating vendors, talent, venues, timelines, and clients simultaneously while managing the inevitable chaos of live production. I built internal systems to handle this complexity.

  • Developed standardized project intake and scoping process for new events
  • Created master production timelines with critical path dependencies mapped
  • Established vendor relationships and negotiation frameworks
  • Built run-of-show (ROS) documentation and day-of briefing systems
  • Post-event review process for continuous operational improvement
  • Client-facing communication cadence and expectation management

Key Learnings

What Running Spades Taught Me

Building and running an event production company is ultimately a crash course in product thinking — every event is a product with a user journey, a delivery timeline, stakeholders, and success metrics. The discipline of managing live events directly shaped how I approach product management work.

  • Systems and documentation are the difference between chaos and excellence at scale
  • Creative vision is only as good as the operation behind it
  • Client trust is built through transparency and proactive communication — not just results
  • Every post-mortem made the next event better; iteration applies to events, not just software
