Context

The Opportunity

Mobile banking has become the primary banking interface for the majority of retail customers. When a platform's mobile experience is confusing, untrustworthy, or frustrating — it directly erodes confidence in the institution itself. For a bank's partner institution, this is an existential UX problem, not just a design inconvenience.

The existing mobile banking app had grown organically over several years — adding features without a coherent UX strategy — resulting in a fragmented, difficult-to-navigate experience that generated high customer service contact rates and poor satisfaction scores. The engagement centered on a principled redesign: simplify, unify, and rebuild trust through design.

"Banking is trust made visible. Every design decision — color, typography, spacing, flow — either builds that trust or erodes it."

My Role

Scope of Engagement

I was engaged to lead the UX design effort — serving as the bridge between user research findings, business requirements from the institutional client, and the design system needed to support the rebuild. My responsibilities spanned from early audit work through validated design deliverables.

  • Conducted comprehensive UX audit of the existing mobile application across all primary user flows
  • Led user research including usability testing sessions with existing customers across demographic groups
  • Synthesized research findings into prioritized opportunity areas and design direction
  • Rebuilt the information architecture from scratch based on observed mental models
  • Designed new interaction patterns for core banking flows: account overview, transfers, payments, and account management
  • Collaborated directly with engineering teams to ensure design feasibility within technical constraints
  • Delivered final design system with component documentation and interaction specs
  • Supported QA review during development to ensure design fidelity
Process

Design Methodology

The redesign followed a structured double-diamond process — but the real work was in the constraints. Financial services UX operates under regulatory requirements, security mandates, and institutional approval processes that don't apply to most product design contexts.

01

Discovery & Audit

Comprehensive audit of the existing app: heuristic evaluation, competitive analysis, and review of customer support data to identify the highest-friction points in the current experience.

02

User Research

Moderated usability testing with existing customers. Observed task completion rates, error rates, and emotional responses across core banking flows to ground design decisions in real behavior.

03

Information Architecture

Card sorting and tree testing exercises to rebuild the app's navigational structure around users' actual mental models — not the bank's internal organizational logic.

04

Design & Iteration

Wireframes → low-fidelity prototype → validated with users → high-fidelity design → stakeholder review → engineering handoff. Multiple iteration cycles with continuous feedback loops.

05

Design System

Built a comprehensive component library aligned with the institution's brand standards and accessibility requirements — designed to scale across the full product surface.

06

Delivery & Handoff

Delivered annotated design specs, component documentation, interaction guidelines, and participated in engineering review to ensure accurate implementation.

Design Principles

What Guided Every Decision

Rather than designing from visual preferences, the redesign was grounded in a set of design principles derived directly from user research — ensuring every tradeoff could be evaluated against a shared framework.

  • Clarity over completeness: Users don't need to see everything at once. Progressive disclosure and clear information hierarchy reduce cognitive load at every screen.
  • Trust is earned in milliseconds: Visual design, motion, and typography work together to signal reliability and institutional credibility before users read a single word.
  • Error prevention over error recovery: Financial errors are high-stakes. The design invests heavily in confirmation flows, clear labels, and safeguards that prevent mistakes rather than just recovering from them.
  • Accessibility as baseline: WCAG compliance is a floor, not a ceiling. Designing for the broadest range of users produces a better experience for everyone.
  • Consistency builds confidence: Predictable patterns reduce anxiety. Users who know where to look — and trust that the experience will behave consistently — engage more deeply with the product.
Constraints

What Made Financial Services UX Unique

Banking UX operates under a layer of constraints that consumer product design rarely encounters — and navigating these constraints while still delivering a meaningfully improved user experience required creative problem-solving and strong stakeholder management.

  • Regulatory requirements governing information disclosure, consent flows, and accessibility
  • Security mandates that shape authentication flows, session management, and data display
  • Legacy system limitations that constrained what was technically feasible in scope
  • Multi-stakeholder approval process across design, legal, compliance, and executive layers
  • Consistency requirements with the parent institution's broader product design language

Reflection

What I Took Away

This engagement taught me how to design inside institutional complexity without losing sight of the user. It sharpened my ability to manage stakeholders across functions, present design rationale to non-design audiences, and navigate the gap between ideal design and real-world delivery constraints. Financial services is one of the most demanding UX environments — and one of the most rewarding when you get it right.
